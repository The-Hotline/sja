Pennsylvania Voter Registration Dates and Deadlines:

  • October 21st, 2024 is the last day to register before the November 5th, 2024 election.
  • Your application must be received by October 21st, 2024 by:
    • Online at the Pennsylvania Voter Registration Application
    • Delivered in person to your county voter registration office (find your county election office here)
    • By mail to your county voter registration office. A blank, fillable voter registration application can be found here.
  • Election Day is November 5th, 2024 and polls in Pennsylvania are open from 7am to 8pm.
  • If you are in line at or before 8pm (when polls close) you will still be allowed to vote.
  • If you plan to vote by absentee or mail-in ballot your application must be received by 5:00pm on October 29th, 2024. Your voted ballot must be returned by 8:00PM on Election Day, November 5th, 2024.
  • You must be a registered voter to submit an application. You can download a mail-in or absentee paper application here OR complete an online application.

Pennsylvania's Address Confidentiality Program:

To protect the information of survivors of domestic violence and their families, survivors can utilize Pennsylvania's Address Confidentiality Program, which provides survivors an authorization card that includes your name, substitute address and participant number. Survivors can use that substitute address for voter registration (and other purposes including drivers' licenses, vehicle registration, school records, utility bills and more). The Address Confidentiality Program is managed by the Pennsylvania Office of Victim Advocate. Survivors can apply online or mail your application. Learn more about this program and how to apply here or call 1-800-563-6399.

Important information for first time voters:

  • If this is the first time you are voting, you must bring a photo or non-photo ID. A list of acceptable IDs can be found here.
  • Use this online tool to find your polling location.
  • Review a sample ballot (and use Survivor Justice Action's candidate guide as you review). Your county election office will have samples you can see in advance.

Check Your Voter Registration:

  • You can check your voter registration status with the Pennsylvania Voter Services.
  • Important for Survivors: If you are already registered, your information may be publicly available. Learn more about Pennsylvania's Address Confidentiality Program and how to protect your information here.
